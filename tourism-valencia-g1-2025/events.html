Events And Festivals

Puhag Festival

The Puhag Festival celebrates Valencia’s long-standing tradition of gathering wild honey, with the word puhag coming from the Visayan term for “collecting honey.” Each year during the town fiesta, the streets fill with dancers dressed as bees and honey gatherers, performing vibrant routines that highlight the community’s connection to nature. The celebration honors Valencia’s beekeeping heritage, showcases cultural pride through street dancing and a grand showdown, and brings locals and visitors together in gratitude for the town’s natural bounty and traditions.

Rambones Festival

The Rambones Festival in Valencia, Negros Oriental celebrates the town’s rambutan and lanzones harvests. The festival features lively street dancing, school performances, pageants, and competitions, all inspired by a local legend of love and unity between rival tribes. It’s a vibrant showcase of Valencia’s culture, agricultural abundance, and community spirit.

Valencia Fiesta

The Fiesta of Valencia, Negros Oriental, celebrated every October 12, honors the town’s patroness, Our Lady of the Abandoned. The festivities begin with a novena Mass and a procession of the Virgin’s statue through the streets, followed by ceremonies at the municipal plaza. The celebration combines faith, culture, and community spirit, featuring religious rituals, music and dance performances, local pageants, trade fairs, and other community events. For residents and visitors alike, the fiesta is a vibrant expression of Valencia’s traditions, hospitality, and gratitude, bringing the town together in celebration of its shared heritage.

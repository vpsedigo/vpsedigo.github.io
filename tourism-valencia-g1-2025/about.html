About The Town

History of Valencia

Valencia was once known as Ermita, meaning “a secluded place,” because it served as a refuge from pirate raids during earlier times. In 1856, during the Spanish colonial era, it was renamed Nueva Valencia in honor of its parish priest from Valencia, Spain.

Later, in 1920, it was renamed Luzuriaga after Don Carlos Ruíz de Luzuriaga, a delegate to the Philippine Legislature, but this name was reverted. Finally, in 1948, the municipality officially took back the name Valencia by virtue of Republic Act No. 252.

Over the years, Valencia has grown steadily in population and development. As of the 2024 census, it has around 39,986 residents.

Places To Visit

Mango Ranch

Mango Ranch, Palinpinon Road, Valencia, Negros Oriental, Philippines

Mango Ranch is a popular spot in Valencia known for its wide open views and relaxing atmosphere. The place has a small waterpark where families can cool off and enjoy the pools. It also has an airpark area with open spaces and fresh mountain air, making it a nice break from the town center. One of the main attractions is the go-kart track, which adds a fun and active experience for visitors of all ages. Overall, Mango Ranch is a good place to enjoy nature, take photos, and have a casual day of fun.

Pulangbato Falls

Barangay Malabo, Valencia, Negros Oriental

Pulangbato Falls is known for its unique red rocks, which give the water and the surrounding cliffs a warm, earthy color. The falls sit in a quiet spot in Valencia, making it a nice place to relax and cool off. The water flows into a calm pool where visitors can swim, and the rising steam from nearby hot spring vents adds to the atmosphere. The mix of red stones, fresh water, and green trees makes the whole area stand out and gives Pulangbato its distinct look. It’s a good stop for anyone who wants both scenery and a peaceful break.

Filipino-Japanese Amity Memorial Shrine

76W5+XRM, Valencia, Negros Oriental

The Japanese shrine in Valencia, Negros Oriental, is formally known as the "Filipino-American-Japanese Amity Shrine", was built in 1997 to commemorate the end of hostilities and honor the soldiers who fought in the battle for the ridge during World War II.

Cata-al World War 2 Museum

Jose Romero Road, Valencia, Negros Oriental

This small museum in Valencia gives a close look at real artifacts left from World War II. Inside, you’ll find weapons, uniforms, old documents, and items recovered from battle sites around Negros Oriental. The collection is well-kept and shows the personal effort of the Cata-al family, who gathered these pieces over many years. It’s a quiet place but rich in stories, and it helps visitors understand what life was like during the war in the region.

Sulfur Vents

Barangay Malabo, Valencia, Negros Oriental

The sulfur vents are a geothermal attraction featuring natural steam vents, mud pools, and a strong sulfur smell, a visible sign of the volcanic energy beneath Mount Talinis. Located roadside, the vents are often visited as a quick stop, sometimes called "Smokey Mountain". The rocks and water in the area are often colored reddish or orange by the sulfur deposits.

Monkey Sanctuary

865G+H8, Valencia, Negros Oriental

The Monkey Sanctuary in Valencia is a peaceful place tucked inside a forested area, known for its long-tailed macaques. The surroundings are quiet and green, which makes the visit feel calm and close to nature. You can see the monkeys moving freely in their natural habitat as you walk through the area. The place also teaches visitors about the importance of protecting wildlife and keeping their environment safe.

Subida Souvenirs

Jose Romero Road, Valencia, Negros Oriental

Subida Souvenirs is a small but charming craft shop in Valencia known for its handmade items created by local Negrense artisans. Inside, you’ll find woven bags, bamboo mugs, wooden toys, home décor, artwork, and other unique pieces that highlight local craftsmanship. The shop isn’t just a place to buy souvenirs — it supports and trains artisans across Negros Oriental, helping preserve traditional Filipino crafts while giving visitors meaningful and culturally rooted pasalubong.

Red Rock Hot Spring

Red Rock Hot Spring is a natural hot spring and relaxation spot known for its rust-colored rocks and warm waters. It features two main pools—one for adults and a shallower one for children—and offers a tranquil escape in a cooler climate.
